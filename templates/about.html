About the Project

Understanding Political Misinformation on Social Media in Kenya

Research Objectives

General Objective

To assess the influence of technology deployment strategies on performance of commercial banks in Kenya.

Specific Objectives

1
To investigate the effect of technology integration practices on the performance of commercial banks in Kenya.
2
To determine the effect of scalability of technology deployment on performance of commercial banks in Kenya.
3
To examine the effect of efficiency of technology management on performance of commercial banks in Kenya.
4
To assess the moderating effect of industry regulations on the relationship between technology deployment strategies and performance of commercial banks in Kenya.

Conceptual Framework

This study integrates the interaction between political misinformation on social media, user content gratification motives, and the application of machine learning models for misinformation detection. It is designed to illustrate the dynamic relationships between these core components within the Kenyan digital political environment, focusing on Twitter (now X) as the primary data source.

Components of the Framework

Political Misinformation on X

This refers to the spread of false, misleading, or manipulated political content through tweets associated with hashtags such as #RutoMustGo and #RejectFinanceBill2024. The misinformation propagates through various formats including text, images, videos, and memes, exploiting the viral nature of social media.

User Content Gratification Motives

Drawing from Uses and Gratifications Theory, this component represents why users engage with political content online. Key motives identified include:

  • Entertainment: Seeking amusement or emotional stimulation.
  • Identity: Reinforcing social or political identity.
  • Connection: Building relationships or social capital within communities.

These motives influence the depth and quality of political discourse, affecting the likelihood of misinformation consumption and dissemination.

Machine Learning-Based Detection

Machine learning algorithms (e.g., Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, Gradient Boosting) are applied to the collected social media data to:

  • Extract linguistic and contextual features
  • Classify tweets as misinformation or factual
  • Predict patterns to mitigate misinformation spread

This technological intervention acts as the mechanism for automating misinformation identification, enhancing scalability and real-time response.

Relationships and Interaction

  • Political misinformation leverages user gratification motives to increase engagement, thereby amplifying reach and impact.
  • User motives can both facilitate the spread of misinformation and serve as indicators for detection models, as linguistic and engagement patterns correlate with gratification types.
  • Machine learning models utilize features derived from content and user behavior to classify misinformation, which can inform platform moderation and policy.

Methodology

Data Collection

Twitter/X data was collected using hashtags related to Kenyan political discourse, including:

#RutoMustGo
#RejectFinanceBill2024
Other politically relevant hashtags

The collected tweets were manually labeled as misinformation or factual content by domain experts.

Analysis Approach

The study employs a mixed-methods approach:

Quantitative

Machine learning models to classify tweets and measure accuracy

Qualitative

Content analysis to identify patterns and themes in misinformation

This approach allows for both automated detection and deeper understanding of misinformation dynamics.
